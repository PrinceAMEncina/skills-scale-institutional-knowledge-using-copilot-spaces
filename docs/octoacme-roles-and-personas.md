# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Project Sponsor

### Role Summary
Project Sponsors provide executive support, secure funding, and align projects with organizational strategy. They serve as the ultimate authority for project decisions and resolve escalated issues that cannot be handled at lower levels.

### Responsibilities
- Provide overall direction and secure project funding
- Align project objectives with organizational strategy and priorities
- Resolve escalated issues and remove organizational roadblocks
- Champion the project with senior leadership and stakeholders
- Review and approve major project milestones and changes
- Ensure resource availability and organizational support

### Goals
- Ensure project delivers strategic value to the organization
- Maintain executive-level visibility and support
- Enable project success through resource allocation and issue resolution

### Typical Communication
- Monthly steering committee meetings
- Ad-hoc escalation resolution sessions
- Milestone reviews and go/no-go decisions
- Executive stakeholder briefings

---

## Change Manager

### Role Summary
Change Managers plan and communicate organizational and process changes, ensuring smooth adoption and minimal disruption. They measure change effectiveness and support teams through transitions.

### Responsibilities
- Develop change management plans and communication strategies
- Assess change impact and identify affected stakeholders
- Coordinate training and support for process or tool changes
- Monitor adoption metrics and address resistance
- Facilitate change readiness assessments
- Document lessons learned from change initiatives

### Goals
- Maximize user adoption and minimize resistance to change
- Ensure smooth transitions with minimal productivity loss
- Build organizational change capability

### Typical Communication
- Change impact assessments and readiness reports
- Training sessions and adoption workshops
- Regular updates to Project Manager and team leads
- Feedback collection and sentiment analysis

---

## Quality Assurance Lead

### Role Summary
Quality Assurance Leads define quality standards, oversee testing efforts, and ensure deliverables meet acceptance criteria. They are accountable for the overall quality of project outputs.

### Responsibilities
- Define and enforce quality standards and testing strategies
- Manage test planning, execution, and defect tracking
- Review acceptance criteria and ensure testability
- Coordinate testing efforts across teams (unit, integration, E2E)
- Report quality metrics and test coverage to stakeholders
- Identify quality risks and recommend mitigation strategies

### Goals
- Ensure all deliverables meet defined quality standards
- Maintain high test coverage and low defect rates
- Enable early defect detection and prevention

### Typical Communication
- Daily test status updates and defect triage
- Weekly quality metrics reports to Project Manager
- Test plan reviews with Developers and Product Managers
- Quality gate approvals before releases

---

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
Business Analysts bridge the gap between business needs and technical implementation. They gather and refine requirements, translate product vision into detailed specifications, and validate deliverables against business objectives.

### Responsibilities
- Elicit and document business requirements from stakeholders
- Translate high-level product vision into detailed user stories
- Facilitate requirements workshops and validation sessions
- Create process flows, data models, and system diagrams
- Ensure requirements are clear, testable, and complete
- Validate deliverables meet business needs and acceptance criteria

### Goals
- Ensure shared understanding between business and technical teams
- Minimize rework through clear, complete requirements
- Bridge communication gaps and align expectations

### Typical Communication
- Requirements workshops with stakeholders and Product Managers
- Clarification sessions with Developers during implementation
- User story refinement and backlog grooming sessions
- Acceptance validation with Product Managers and QA

---

## Subject Matter Expert (SME)

### Role Summary
Subject Matter Experts provide specialized domain or technical knowledge to guide project decisions. They review deliverables for compliance, best practices, and technical accuracy.

### Responsibilities
- Provide domain expertise and industry best practices guidance
- Review technical designs and deliverables for accuracy
- Identify compliance, regulatory, or technical risks
- Advise on architectural decisions and technology choices
- Support knowledge transfer and documentation efforts
- Validate solutions against domain-specific requirements

### Goals
- Ensure technical and domain accuracy of solutions
- Minimize risks through expert guidance and review
- Transfer specialized knowledge to the project team

### Typical Communication
- On-demand consultation sessions with Project Manager and Developers
- Technical design reviews and architecture discussions
- Risk assessments and mitigation recommendations
- Documentation reviews and knowledge sharing sessions
